Cast Iron Skillet Steak Recipe

The best way to cook a steak

Who doesn't love a nice medium-rare steak with an outer crust forged in the intense heat of cast iron skillet to capture rich umami characters with hints of charred caramelization?

The cast iron skillet method of cooking a steak is foolproof and the heat distribution ensures that the interior remains moist and flavorful, while the outer crust is a crunchy, golden-brown. Each bite delivers a depth of taste that evolves as you savor it, with the interplay of umami, smokiness, and the meat's natural essence creating a well-rounded and satisfying flavor profile The steak's juiciness is a testament to the skillful cooking method, so hurry and grab your ingredients to great your taste buds with a symphony of flavors.

Ingredients needed

  • Steak, Ribeye steak are best and will retain their juicy flavor after high temps
  • Butter
  • Olive Oil
  • Garlic
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Thyme
  • Rosemary

Instructions

  1. Set steak out at room temperature for 30 minutes and pat dry
  2. Season well with salt and pepper
  3. Heat cast iron skillet with olive oil over medium-high head until it shimmers
  4. Lay steak in and lower heat to medium. Cook until a deep golden brown crust forms, about 2 minutes
  5. Flip the steak and add butter, thyme, and garlic
  6. Tilt the skillet and baste the steak with the melted butter until cooked
  7. Let steak rest for at least 5 minutes before slicing and serving
